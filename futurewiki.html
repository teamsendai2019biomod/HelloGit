Future



Gel Muscle can shrink and expand in response to the supply of corresponding hairpin DNAs. It is powered by Reversible Hybridization Chain Reaction (RHCR). RHCR is the improvement from HCR with addition it is reversible. Extending and regressing reaction take place repeatedly until all the monomer hairpins in the solution are consumed, which enables extraordinary swelling/shrinking rate of the gel.

The RHCR which imitate the actin dynamics can open up the application for programmable actin function in the cell. The actin in a cell has multitude of functions, such as intracellular transport, adhesion and contraction, membrane dynamics and migration, cytokinesis and cell-cell contact regulation, polarity and cell shape control. The ability of large shrinkage/expansion Gel Muscle has potential applications in the areas of healthcare and biomedicine. For instances, the gel can be utilized as a biosensor that swells or shrinks depending on the environment 1.

Moreover, the hydrogel has unique properties, such as high-water content, softness, flexibility, and biocompatibility. These characteristics that resemble living tissue opens up an application for tissue engineering and wound dressings 2.

From a larger term perspective, Gel Muscle can be applied for programmable biopolymer, artificial endoskeleton, and so on. It might even lead to super-artificial endoskeleton of soft-based robot like a hybrid robot. Biohybrid robot is the new futuristic robot that uses living tissue as their compartment other than plastics and metal 3 . Because Muscle Gel is mimicking the actin, an important part of the muscle, we expect this project can contribute to the development of biohybrid robot in the future.
