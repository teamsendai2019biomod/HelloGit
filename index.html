Gel Muscle

~powered by DNA~

Background



The existing robotics has developed in the direction of making more accurate and more predictable systems that consist rigid bodies. However, it does not have flexibility and thereby nature often surpasses such rigid robots in performance. Having this as background, there has been the trend of soft robotics which aims to imitate the natural world [1].
As a material for soft robots, hydrogel has an excellent value in terms of versatile functionalities along with its biological compatibility and environmental responsiveness. For instance, various measures such as pH control or thermal modulation are known as methods of size regulation of such gels [2]. Among them, Hybridization Chain Reaction (HCR) which is known to have an ability to swell hydrogel hundredfold in volume by adding single- stranded DNAs with specific sequences is of our interest because of its high programmability [3]. The HCR used in our project is able to extend double stranded DNA complex without losing the connectivity between the crosslinking points throughout the reaction. The gel can swell by elongating the crosslink made of the DNA complex between the matrix polymers. This dynamic volumetric change of hydrogel is expected to be applied to metamorphic robots or actuators such as muscles of soft robots.

Problem



Actin is polymer which configures muscles and it has both reactions of polymerization and depolymerization. The dynamic swelling of hydrogel is feasible through a DNA specific reaction called HCR with extraneous stimuli. However, to apply this size change to various measures such as an actuator, it is necessary to add an element of shrinking, not just swelling, so that the overall reactions become reversible (fig. 1).
To realize such reversible regulation, it is needed to control the reactions of elongation and regression in response to external signals. Here, we design a swelling and shrinking system of hydrogel which can keep its crosslinking points by adding signal DNA from outside. Because the whole reaction takes place in a hydrogel in a buffer solution, the interface between hydrogel and the external environment will be easily implemented.
