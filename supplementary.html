Condition Comparisons

In order to progress HCR more, we changed the experimental condition. At this point, we changed the incubation time, thermo condition. The results are shown in the figure below.

Incubation time

We wanted to confirm the effect of incubation time on HCR. Therefore, we performed the experiment with two varieties of incubation time, one-hour incubation and overnight incubation. We expected that the longer the incubation process is, the more we can find the bands near the well.

Fig.S1 Examination of effect of incubation time(1hr at room temperature)


Compared to the one incubated overnight(Fig.1), there was no such big difference between these two samples. From this result, we concluded that 1 hour incubation is enough for hybridization of our DNA components. Thus, we decided to incubate the prepared samples for 1 hour before electrophoresis.


Temperature condition

In general, at higher temperature, hybridization reaction will be accelerated. Thus, we tried to confirm the effect of temperature. (Fig.3)

Compared to Fig.S1, we observed more bands near the well. Lane 13(C+H1+H2) and 15(A’+R’+H1+H2) especially showed Smeared band, which matches S. Venkataraman (2007). However, smeared bands are barely seen(Fig.2), suggesting that bands that formed near the well are unstable structures, which will transform into more stable structures.

Hairpin DNAs concentration (Fig.4)

We thought the result (Fig.4A) was due to the lack of hairpin DNA compared to S. Venkataraman (2007) . So, we conducted the experiment to confirm the effect of higher hairpin DNAs concentration. As a result, there is no big change between the one with higher hairpin DNA concentration and the one with lower hairpin DNA concentration (Fig.4B, lane 14 & 17) . Thus, for hairpin DNA, 4 times as much concentration as the initiator is still enough for hybridization reaction.

Confirmation of reaction limitation by R’

Furthermore, we tried to confirm R’ effect on delaying the HCR by restricting the toehold length of R’.(Fig.4B) As a result, the one without R’ doesn’t differ from the one mixed altogether, suggesting that R’ doesn’t limit the hybridization in our DNA samples.
