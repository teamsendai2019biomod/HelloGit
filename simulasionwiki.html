Simulation about swelling reactions of hydrogel

Purpose

HCR is an effective method to swell hydrogel, but there is a disadvantage that the reactions take up much time. Also, it is difficult to actually confirm the swelling o f such hydrogels because the observation time can be extremely long. Thereby, we decided to s imulate the alteration in concentration of DNA elongation reaction and the velocity of the expansion rate.


Simulation about serial concentration change First, the chemical reaction formulae that are used in the simulation is below:

[ C1]+[H1] k→ [ C2] (1)

[ C2]+[H2] k→ [ C3] (2)

[ C3]+[H1] k→ [ C4] (3)

[ C4]+[H2] k→ [ C5] (4)

[ Cn-1]+[H2] k→ [ Cn] (n-1)

[ Cn]+[H2] k→ [ Cn+1] (n)

Here, H1, H2, C1, C2, …, Cn+1 are DNA molecules below:

Fig. 1 The DNA molecules related to the simulation

C is a crosslinking DNA which forms a bridge between acrylamide, and k is the velocity rate.

Equation (1) is a reaction that H1 binds to crosslinking DNA. Here, the initial crosslinking DNA is named C1, and the crosslinking DNA produce by the binding of H1 is named C2. As a result, C2 is twice as long as C1. Equation (2) is the further reaction of C2 which H2 is newly bound. Here, C3 is newly formed DNA which becomes threefold in length by the binding of H2.


In our simulation, the reactions explained above are considered to be one cycle, and by repeating this cycle, DNAs elongate longer and longer. Because these reactions proceed endlessly as long as both hairpin structures H1 and H2 exist, the amounts of the calculations in the program increase infinitely if without any regulations. To prevent it, we decided to regulate the maximum length of DNA which is produced as a result of successive reactions. The maximum length is defined to be 2n+1 times longer than the initial one when the cycle explained above happens up to n times (n; arbitrary number). For instance, if n=2, the cycles stops after equation (2), and the longest DNA produced is 5 times longer than its initial length. In this instance, the maximum number of hairpin structures that can bind to one DNA is 4 in total.

[C1] + [H1] → [C2] [C2] + [H2] → [C3] }1cycle
[C3] + [H1] → [C4] [C4] + [H2] → [C5] }2cycle

・・・・・・・・・

From these equations, we postulate that up to 100 cycles happen, and proceed the simulation by solving multiple differential equations below:


The rate equations

d[H1] dt = α(-k[C1][H1] - k[C3][H1] - ・・・・・・・ - k[C2i-1][H1] - ・・・・・・・ - k[C199][H1])
d[H2] dt = α(-k[C2][H2] - k[C4][H2] - ・・・・・・・ - k[C2i][H2] - ・・・・・・・ - k[C200][H2])

(i: arbitrary number, 1≤i≤n).



d[C1] dt = k[H1][H2]
d[C2] dt = k[C1][H1] - k[C2][H2] d[C3] dt = k[C2][H1] - k[C3][H2] }1cycle

d[C4] dt = k[C3][H1] - k[C4][H2] d[C5] dt = k[C4][H1] - k[C5][H2] }2cycle

・・・・・・・・・・


d[C2i] dt = k[C2i-1][H1] - k[C2i][H2] d[C2i+1] dt = k[C2i][H1] - k[C2i+1][H2] }icycle

・・・・・・・・・・


d[C198] dt = k[C197][H1] - k[C198][H2] d[C199] dt = k[C198][H1] - k[C197][H2] }99cycle

d[C200] dt = k[C199][H1] - k[C200][H2] }100cycle
d[C200] dt = k[C200][H1]

Here, is the ratio of the volume of the hydrogel to the volume of the solution around the gel. Although the concentration change of hairpin structures H1 and H2 happens only inside the hydrogel, it is still shown in these equation when we consider the concentration change in both hydrogel and solution because these hairpin structures also exist in the surrounding solution. To start the simulation, the initial concentration of crosslinking DNA and the hairpin structures H1 and H2 are set to 1.1 mmol/L and 20 μmol/L respectively to proceed the experiment in the equal condition with the reproductive one. Plus, according to the figure in the previous study, we carefully chose consistent k and , which is k=3.3×102 L/mol, =10-4. We ran the calculation on silab.

Simulation of expansion rate Here is explanations about simulation of expansion rate of the gel. First of all, we presuppose that the extension of DNA contributes to the hydrogel swelling.

ΔL L = ΔH × ∑ n = 2 201 (n-1) × [Cn] × Na × Vg × β × γ L

Here is the equation leads the expansion rate. In this equation above, n is natural number, ∆H=8 nm, which shows the elongation realized by the binding of hairpin structures, Na is Avogadro number,

Vg = 1×1×0.136(mm3)

is the volume of the hydrogel, β = 1 12+12+0.1362 is the ratio of the diagonal length to the side length of the gel, and L is the side length. ∑ n = 2 201 | C n | is the sum of concentration of each crosslinking DNA produced throughout the reactions. Simulation about shrinking reactions of hydrogel

Concentration-time simulation

Purpose

To investigate if the elongated DNAs are theoretically regressed by RHCR (Reverse HCR) when EH1 (H1 eraser) and EH1-H1 releasers of it is added to the reaction system, we ran the simulation by solving ordinary differential equations about chemical reaction formulae below:


[C2n+1]+[EH1]+[D] K→ [C2n]+[E]+[H2](1) [C2n]+[EH1]+[D] K`→ [C2n-1]+[E]+[H2](2)

・・・・・・


[C5]+[EH1]+[D] K`→ [C4]+[E]+[H2](n-3) [C4]+[EH1]+[D] → [C3]+[E]+[H2](n-2)

Here, k’ is the reaction rate constant, and EH1, D, E are DNA molecules below:

Equation (1) shows a reaction which one H1 and one H2 dissociate from initial crosslinking DNA bound by n-H1 and n-H2, 2n hairpin structures in total. In this equation, the very first state of DNA with 2n hairpin structures is named C2n+1, and in the same way, the DNA with (2n-2) hairpin structures is named C2n. When the reactions happen in the equation (1) and (2) are considered to be one cycle, DNA becomes shorter and shorter by going through the cycles repeatedly. Looking it in the same way, equation (n-2) shows a reaction which one H1 and one H2 dissociate from crosslinking DNA and it backs to the initial state before beginning elongation reactions. Here, C3 is crosslinking DNA bound by one H1 and one H2. The successive reactions stops here because both EH1 and EH1-H1 releasers do not react with DNAs shorter than C. For instance, if n=2, the cycles stops after equation (2), and the length of the possible shortest DNA is 3 times longer than the initial crosslinking DNA. At this time, one crosslinking DNA is bound by 2 hairpin structures in total.
